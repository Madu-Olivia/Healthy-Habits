Healthy Study Habits

Staying productive as a student isn't just about long study hours- it's about studying smart and staying healthy. These habits will help you focus better, feel less tired, and keep your mind active and balanced throughout the day.

Study Routine Time

  • Total: Approximately 2 hours daily
  • Study Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Breaks & Refresh Time:30 minutes

Ideal Study Time vs. Break Time

Research shows that studying in short bursts (about 25-50 minutes) followed by a 5-10 minutes break helps your brain stay focused and remember more. Try using the Pomodoro technique - study, pause, refresh and repeat.

Brain-Booosting Snacks

Keep your brain energized with light snacks like nuts, yogurt, or fruits. Avoid caffeine - they might give you quick energy but can make you tired later.

Posture Tips While Studying

Sit upright with your back supported and your screen or book at eye level. Bad posture can cause back or neck pain and make studying uncomfortable over time.

Importance Of Sleep

Good sleep is like "saving progress" for your brain. Aim for 7-8 hours each night to help your mind store what you've learned and wake up refreshed for new lessons.
